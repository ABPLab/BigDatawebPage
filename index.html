Big Data for Good

We use Artificial Intelligence
for good


At the Affiliative Behaviour and Physiology Lab and at the Social and Affective Neuroscience Lab we employ novel Artificial Intelligence technqique and large scale Cloud Computing models for the analysis of neurophysiological signals and behavioural data.

Our practices are based on an Open Science approach, where data are made available, togheter with our softwares and homemade analysis tools, to allow for reproducibility of our results and to demonstrate the quality of our work.

Thanks to our international collaborations, we create new knowledge trough the application of novel Machine Learning and Neural Networks models to different types of big data.

Child Mortality in Low and Middle Income Countries

Child mortality still affects 3.9% of children below five years old worldwide with a large and worrisome disparity between high- (0.9%) and low-income countries (7.5% in African countries). The objective of the study is to identify risk factors associated with Child Mortality, trough the analysis of the MICS (Multiple Indicator Cluster Survey), a dataset created by UNICEF.

We adopted the Micro-Array Quality Control Data Analysis Plan (MAQC-DAP) on Random Forest models to identify Child Mortality's Risk Factors.

Our results reveal how the age of the mother, the availability of electricy within the household, and the highest level of education of the mother are amongst the top factors that have an influence on child mortality.
